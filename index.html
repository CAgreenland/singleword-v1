single word FOCUS

One word. One fixation.

Your eyes stop jumping between lines, margins, and headings. The next word appears on its own — exactly where you are already looking — so reading feels like following a single, steady target instead of scanning a whole page.

Minimal stage FOCAL POINT
RHYTHM WPM

Read at your pace.

Slow down for dense material, speed up for lighter text. You set the cadence — the reader follows, word by word, without losing your place. Adjust words per minute anytime; the flow stays smooth and predictable.

Your rhythm SPEED
PIVOT

A steady anchor letter.

Each word has a highlighted centre letter so your gaze has a predictable place to land. That anchor stays in the same visual lane as words change — less darting around, more actual reading.

Central anchor PIVOT
CALM CORE

No competing lines.

No scroll, no sidebars, no notifications asking for attention. Just the sentence you came to finish — one beat at a time. The stage stays quiet so your mind can stay on the words, not the chrome.

Reader core QUIET
ATTENTION ADHD FRIENDLY

Easier to stay with long text.

A steady external pace lowers the “where was I?” moments on dense or difficult material, so attention follows intention — not the layout. Helpful for long articles, study sessions, and anyone who prefers a calmer, guided read.

Made for focus STEADY
SOURCE Use your own source

Paste or upload anything.

Articles, study notes, chapters, plain paragraphs — the reader adapts to whatever you want to finish today, and picks up where you left off. Paste from the clipboard or open common formats so your source stays yours — no extra toolchain required.

Any text ANY TIME
LIBRARY RESUME

Your place, saved on this device.

Uploads and (with a paid plan) pasted text can live in a simple library with your last position. Open the reader later and continue from the same word — no digging through tabs or PDFs to find where you stopped.

Pick up again SAVED
COMFORT CONTROLS

Dial in speed, size, and colour.

Set reading speed, font size, and colours for the word and the centre letter. Keyboard shortcuts keep play, pause, and stepping under your fingers — so you can tune the look and feel for long sessions or quick passes.
